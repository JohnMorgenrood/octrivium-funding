**Date:** November 23, 2025

**Model:** Revenue-Based Investment Platform for South African SMEs

## Executive Summary

Octrivium operates in a high-potential market (South African SME financing) with a differentiated model (revenue-based investments vs. equity crowdfunding). However, to achieve scale, institutional credibility, and potential acquisition value, the platform requires significant improvements across transparency, trust architecture, deal quality presentation, investor education, regulatory positioning, and technical performance.

**Key Strategic Imperatives:**
1. **Transparency & Trust:** Publish portfolio performance metrics, case studies, and deal underwriting criteria
2. **Risk Management:** Implement robust risk frameworks and clear disclosure mechanisms
3. **Investor Experience:** Simplify onboarding, add portfolio dashboards, enable recurring investments
4. **Deal Quality:** Strengthen deal flow, underwriting standards, and narrative presentation
5. **Regulatory Clarity:** Clear FSCA positioning, compliance messaging, and investor protection
6. **Technical Excellence:** Improve speed, mobile optimization, accessibility, and conversion rates
7. **Differentiation:** Build strong ESG/impact narrative and community engagement

### Critical Weaknesses

âŒ **No Track Record Visibility** - Zero published performance data, case studies, or portfolio metrics  
âŒ **Limited Deal Flow Transparency** - No clear criteria for what makes a "good deal"  
âŒ **Ambiguous Risk Framework** - Investors don't see default rates, recovery processes, or risk scoring  
âŒ **No Institutional Positioning** - Unclear if FSCA-regulated, how funds are held, investor protections

### Recommended Model Enhancements

#### 1.1 Revenue Model Optimization
- **Tiered Investor Accounts:** Free (1 deal), Premium (R199/mo, 5 deals + analytics), Institutional (custom)
- **Performance Fees:** Consider 1-2% carry on returns above 15% IRR (align incentives)
- **SME SaaS Upsell:** Charge businesses R499/mo for accounting software + priority deal listing
- **Deal Origination Fees:** 2-3% upfront fee from businesses (standard in SA market)
- **Secondary Market:** Enable investor liquidity via marketplace (take 1% transaction fee)

#### 1.2 Deal Structure Best Practices
- **Revenue Share Range:** 2-8% of monthly revenue until 1.2-1.8x repaid
- **Term Limits:** 12-36 months maximum (reduces investor risk)
- **Minimum Revenue Threshold:** R50k+ monthly recurring revenue (predictability)
- **Underwriting Criteria:** 
  - 12+ months operating history
  - Positive cash flow last 6 months
  - Verified bank statements via Stitch integration
  - Credit bureau checks (Experian/TransUnion)
  - Industry risk scoring (hospitality = higher risk than SaaS)

## 7. Risk Management & Regulatory Compliance

### Current Regulatory Landscape (South Africa)

**Key Acts:**
- **FAIS Act (2002):** Regulates financial advice and intermediary services
- **Financial Markets Act (2012):** Governs crowdfunding and securities
- **FSCA Guidance Note (2020):** Crowdfunding platforms must register as Financial Service Providers (FSPs)
- **FICA (2001):** Anti-money laundering, KYC requirements

### Compliance Checklist

#### 7.1 FSCA Registration (Critical)
- [ ] Apply for FSP license (costs ~R50k-150k)
- [ ] Appoint compliance officer
- [ ] Submit crowdfunding platform application
- [ ] Annual FSCA audits and reporting

**If Not Yet Registered:**
- Clearly state: "Octrivium facilitates connections between investors and businesses. We do not provide financial advice. Seek independent counsel."
- Add prominent risk warnings on every deal page

#### 7.2 Risk Disclosure Framework
**Mandatory Warnings:**
- "High-risk investment: You may lose all capital invested"
- "No government protection (not covered by FSCS)"
- "Illiquid: Difficult to sell before repayment term ends"
- "Past performance does not guarantee future results"
- "Diversify: Do not invest more than 10% of your net worth"

**Add Risk Quiz:**
- "Have you invested in alternative assets before?"
- "Can you afford to lose this capital?"
- "Do you understand revenue-based investing?"
- Score <70% â†’ Show educational content before allowing investment

#### 7.3 Default & Recovery Process
**Publish Clear Process:**
1. **Missed Payment** (Day 1) - Automated reminder to business
2. **Grace Period** (Days 1-14) - Account manager contacts business
3. **Restructuring Attempt** (Days 15-30) - Negotiate revised terms
4. **Legal Notice** (Days 31-60) - Formal demand letter
5. **Collections** (Days 61+) - Partner with debt recovery firm
6. **Investor Updates** - Monthly emails on status, recovery amount

**Historical Recovery Rate:** "Octrivium has recovered 68% of defaulted capital within 12 months"

#### 7.4 Fund Segregation (Build Trust)
**Best Practice:**
- Investor funds held in separate trust account (not Octrivium's operating account)
- Audited annually by Big 4 firm
- Clear on website: "Your capital is held in a segregated trust account with [Bank Name]"
